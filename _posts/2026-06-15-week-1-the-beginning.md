I'll be honest — I almost skipped Monday's session.

I had already packed my gym bag the night before, set two alarms, told three people I was starting this plan. And still, when 5 PM rolled around, I sat at my desk thinking of reasons not to go.

But I went. And that's the whole story of week 1.

## What I actually did

I followed the plan almost exactly. Monday was full body strength — squats, bench press, lat pulldown, lunges, and planks. I used lighter weights than the plan suggested because my form was shaky on squats and I didn't want to hurt myself on day one.

Tuesday was HIIT. The sprint intervals nearly killed me. Six rounds of 30-second sprints and I was done — completely drenched, embarrassingly out of breath. But I finished.

I missed Friday's session because of a late work call. So 4 out of 5 this week. Not perfect, but I'm counting it.

## The diet reality

Breakfast was easy. The eggs + toast combo takes 10 minutes and keeps me full until 10:30. No complaints.

Lunch is where I slipped. Wednesday I ate rice + dal + sabzi as planned. Thursday I was in a meeting that ran through lunch and grabbed whatever was available — biryani, samosa, the works. It happens. I didn't beat myself up about it.

The post-dinner walk is becoming my favourite part of the day. 10 minutes after dinner, just walking around the colony, no phone. It's genuinely relaxing.

## Honest thoughts

The soreness on Wednesday was real. My legs were so sore from Monday's squats that climbing stairs hurt. I did some light stretching and it helped.

The most surprising thing: I slept better this week than I have in months. I don't know if it's the exercise, the reduced screen time, or the post-dinner walk — probably all three.

**Week 2 starts Monday. I'm going.**
