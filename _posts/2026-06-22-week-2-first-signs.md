All 5 sessions done. First time in my adult life I completed a full week of planned workouts.

I'm writing this on Sunday evening, legs sore from Saturday's walk (yes, even the recovery walk made me sore — that tells you where I started). But the numbers moved this week and that changes everything mentally.

## The workouts

I added weight to the squats on Thursday compared to Monday. Not much — just 5 kg — but it felt significant. The HIIT sessions went from "I might die" to "this is hard but manageable." The sprint intervals still feel brutal at round 6, 7, 8 but I'm not stopping anymore.

The plank went from 30 seconds (barely) to 45 seconds both sets. Small win.

## Diet — much better this week

I meal prepped on Sunday evening. Cooked dal, boiled eggs, chopped vegetables. It took 45 minutes and made every weekday meal decision automatic. This is the real hack.

Zero slips this week. The pre-workout banana + black coffee combo is genuinely effective — I had noticeably more energy during the HIIT session on Tuesday when I remembered it vs Friday when I skipped the coffee.

## The numbers

| Metric | Week 1 | Week 2 | Change |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Weight | 78.4 kg | 77.8 kg | −0.6 kg ✅ |
| Waist | 92 cm | 91 cm | −1 cm ✅ |
| Energy | 6/10 | 7/10 | +1 ✅ |
| Sessions | 4/5 | 5/5 | ✅ |

The 1 cm off the waist in one week surprised me. I know it's partly water weight and reduced bloating from cutting out packaged snacks. But I'll take it.

## What I noticed this week

The post-lunch walk has become something I look forward to. 10–15 minutes, headphones in, just moving. My afternoon energy dip (the 2–3 PM crash I've had for years) was noticeably less severe both days I walked after lunch.

Desk habits: I'm standing up every 45 minutes. It feels weird and slightly annoying but I'm doing it.

Sleep is still better. I'm getting 7.5 hours on average vs maybe 6 before.

**Week 3 is where the plan says energy levels rise significantly. Let's see.**
